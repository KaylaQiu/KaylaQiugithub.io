Kayla Qiu is a motivated and goal-driven high school student from Temple City, California, known for her strong work ethic, enthusiasm for learning, and commitment to community service. A junior at Temple City High School, Kayla maintains a solid academic performance with a GPA of 3.7. Her coursework includes challenging subjects such as Biology, Chemistry Honors, and AP Statistics, which reflect her interest in science and analytical thinking. These academic pursuits align with her aspiration to apply her skills and knowledge in cancer research, specifically through a summer internship at the City of Hope, where she hopes to make meaningful contributions. Outside the classroom, Kayla is an active participant in school life and community events. She is a member of both the Art Club and the Dungeons & Dragons Club, demonstrating her creative talents and collaborative spirit. Her extracurricular involvement extends to athletics as well—she is a dedicated member of the high school cross-country team. Training on both school tracks and mountain trails, she has developed endurance, discipline, and team-building skills that have shaped her character. Kayla’s passion for service is evident through her extensive volunteer work. She has participated in a range of community service projects that highlight her willingness to help and her strong sense of responsibility. She volunteered at the 2022 Cloverly Elementary Father and Daughter Dance, where she assisted with setup and cleanup, helping ensure a successful event. At the Temple City Camellia Festival, she engaged in fundraising for Relay for Life by selling confetti eggs and also offered face painting services at the Oak Avenue Middle School booth. In addition, Kayla served as an usher and setup assistant at the 2022 Pasadena Symphony and Pops summer concerts held at the LA County Arboretum, further showcasing her dependability and organizational abilities. More recently, in 2023, she contributed to the West California Academy of Art and Design’s Lunar New Year celebration at the Santa Anita Racetrack by helping children with painting activities—an experience that combined her love for art with her desire to give back to the community.
